# The Attention Problem Nobody in B2B Is Talking About

**Author:** Amanda Natividad
**Date:** 2024-12-11
**Likes:** ~2,500 | **Comments:** ~200
**Post Type:** Text post

---

B2B marketers obsess over reach.

"How do we get more impressions?"
"How do we grow our LinkedIn following?"

These are the wrong questions.

The right question: How do we earn attention from the right 500 people?

SparkToro data shows this consistently:

Most B2B buying decisions are influenced by a small, concentrated set of voices your buyers already trust. Not by the brand with the most followers.

Your ICP reads the same 3 newsletters.
They listen to the same 2 podcasts.
They follow the same 15 people on LinkedIn.

If you are not in that trusted set — you are invisible to your buyers, regardless of follower count.

The LinkedIn organic strategy that works in B2B is not "grow a big audience."

It is: "become one of the 15 accounts my ICP actually reads."

That requires:
→ Deep specificity (not general marketing content)
→ A genuine point of view (not neutral takes)
→ Consistency in one narrow lane (not variety)

Reach for depth, not width.

**Why collected:** Uses SparkToro research to reframe the goal of LinkedIn organic strategy — from follower growth to becoming a trusted voice within a specific ICP's information diet.
