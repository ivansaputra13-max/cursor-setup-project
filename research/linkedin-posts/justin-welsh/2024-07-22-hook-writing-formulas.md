I've analyzed 1,000+ LinkedIn posts.

The #1 difference between posts that get 50 views and posts that get 50,000 views?

The hook. Those first two lines decide everything.

Here are the 5 hook formulas I use most:

**1. The bold claim** — "Most LinkedIn advice is wrong."
**2. The counterintuitive** — "Posting every day hurt my growth."
**3. The specific result** — "I generated 180K last year from one LinkedIn post format."
**4. The direct question** — "Why do some LinkedIn posts get 1,000 comments while others get 3?"
**5. The relatable struggle** — "I used to spend 2 hours writing a post and get 12 likes."

Practice writing 5 hooks for every post.
Use the best one. Delete the other four.

Your hook is your headline. Treat it that way.
