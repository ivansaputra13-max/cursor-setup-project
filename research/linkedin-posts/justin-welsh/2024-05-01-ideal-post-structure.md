# The Exact Structure of Every LinkedIn Post That Performs

**Author:** Justin Welsh
**Date:** 2024-05-01
**Likes:** ~5,100 | **Comments:** ~380
**Post Type:** Text post

---

After 4 years and 1,000+ posts, here's the structure I use every time:

**Line 1:** Hook — bold claim or counterintuitive statement
**Line 2:** Create curiosity or tension (do NOT resolve it yet)
[blank line — forces "see more" click]

**Lines 3–5:** Context. Why should they care?

**Lines 6–15:** The value. List, story, or framework. Short sentences. White space. Easy to scan.

**Lines 16–18:** The insight. One takeaway.

**Last line:** CTA or open question that invites a comment.

Most people write the value first.
The best writers write the hook first.

Because if no one clicks "see more," the value doesn't matter.
