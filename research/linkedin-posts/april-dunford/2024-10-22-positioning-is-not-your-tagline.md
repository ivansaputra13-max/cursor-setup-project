# Positioning Is Not Your Tagline

**Author:** April Dunford
**Date:** 2024-10-22
**Likes:** ~3,100 | **Comments:** ~250
**Post Type:** Text post

---

Most B2B companies confuse positioning with their tagline.

Your tagline is what you say.
Your positioning is what you believe about where you compete and who you serve.

Here's why this matters for your LinkedIn content:

If your positioning is unclear, your content will be unclear.
You'll write posts that try to appeal to everyone.
They'll resonate with no one.

Strong positioning means you know:
→ Who your best customers actually are (not "anyone with a budget")
→ What alternatives they'd use if you didn't exist
→ Why you win against those alternatives for that specific customer
→ What value they get that they can't get anywhere else

When you know all four of those things, writing LinkedIn content becomes easy.

Every post is a different angle on the same clear answer to: "Why should this specific person care about what we do?"

Weak positioning = vague content = low engagement = no pipeline.
Strong positioning = specific content = the right people saying "this is exactly for me."

Fix your positioning first. The content follows.
