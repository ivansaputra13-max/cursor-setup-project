# The Switch Moment — Why Buyers Finally Say Yes

**Author:** Katelyn Bourgoin
**Date:** 2024-11-18
**Likes:** ~2,200 | **Comments:** ~185
**Post Type:** Text post

---

Most B2B marketers think buyers decide because of features or price.

They don't.

Buyers decide because of a "switch moment" — when the pain of staying the same finally outweighs the risk of changing.

What triggers a switch moment:

**A passive push:** Something gets worse. The spreadsheet crashes one too many times. The manual process costs them a deal.

**An active pull:** Someone shows them a better way exists. A peer recommends a tool. They read a LinkedIn post that makes them think "I didn't know this was possible."

Your LinkedIn content can be that active pull.

The best B2B LinkedIn posts describe the moment just before the switch — with enough specificity that your buyer reads it and thinks: "This person understands exactly what I'm going through."

When a buyer feels truly understood, they are primed to change.

Ask before every post: "Is this describing the world my buyer lives in right now?"

Write for the world they live in. The switch will happen naturally.
