# Why Your LinkedIn Content Isn't Converting

**Author:** Katelyn Bourgoin
**Date:** 2024-09-30
**Likes:** ~2,400 | **Comments:** ~200
**Post Type:** Text post

---

Most B2B marketers think their LinkedIn content isn't working because the writing is bad.

Usually that's not the problem.

The problem is they don't understand the buyer's job-to-be-done.

Every piece of content your ICP engages with is helping them do a "job."

Not "learn about CRM software."

A real human job like:
→ "Help me look smart in front of my CEO"
→ "Give me ammunition to convince my team to change"
→ "Help me feel like I'm not the only one struggling with this"
→ "Show me I'm making the right decision"

When your LinkedIn content speaks to those underlying jobs — not just the surface topic — engagement goes up. Trust goes up. Conversion goes up.

The test: read your last 5 LinkedIn posts and ask:
"What job is this doing for my reader?"

If the answer is "teaching them about my product" — that's not a job anyone hired content to do.

If the answer is "making them feel seen and understood" — now you're getting somewhere.
