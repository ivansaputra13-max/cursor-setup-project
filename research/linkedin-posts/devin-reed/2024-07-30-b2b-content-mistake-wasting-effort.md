# The B2B Content Mistake That Wastes 80% of Your Effort

**Author:** Devin Reed
**Date:** 2024-07-30
**Likes:** ~2,300 | **Comments:** ~190
**Post Type:** Text post

---

Most B2B content teams spend energy answering questions buyers are already asking.

That sounds smart. It's actually a trap.

If buyers are already asking the question on Google, they're already in-market.
You're competing with 50 other companies for the same searcher.

The higher-leverage move: answer questions buyers don't know to ask yet.

I call this "teaching the category."

Instead of: "How to choose a revenue intelligence platform" (answering their question)

Write: "Why most sales teams are measuring the wrong thing in pipeline reviews" (creating the question)

The first post reaches buyers already evaluating solutions.
The second reaches buyers before they start evaluating — and positions you as the company that taught them how to think.

On LinkedIn this is even more powerful because:
→ Your competitors aren't doing it (they're focused on SEO)
→ It creates a differentiated perspective, not a commodity post
→ It builds trust that makes buyers disqualify competitors before speaking to them

The best B2B LinkedIn content doesn't answer demand.
It creates it.
