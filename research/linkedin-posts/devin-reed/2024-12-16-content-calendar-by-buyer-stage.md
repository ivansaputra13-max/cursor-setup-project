# Your LinkedIn Content Calendar Is Wrong

**Author:** Devin Reed
**Date:** 2024-12-16
**Likes:** ~2,900 | **Comments:** ~245
**Post Type:** Text post

---

Most B2B content calendars are organized by topic or by date.

Neither is the right organizing principle.

The right organizing principle: buyer stage.

**Tier 1 — Problem-aware content (40% of posts)**
For buyers who know they have a problem but don't know solutions exist.
Goal: make them feel understood.
Example: "3 signs your sales team is flying blind on pipeline health"

**Tier 2 — Solution-aware content (40% of posts)**
For buyers who know solutions exist but haven't committed.
Goal: teach them how to evaluate. Frame the decision in your favor.
Example: "What to ask a revenue intelligence vendor before you sign anything"

**Tier 3 — Product-aware content (20% of posts)**
For buyers actively evaluating.
Goal: remove objections. Show proof.
Example: "How [customer] reduced sales cycle length by 30% in 90 days"

Most B2B SaaS companies post 80% Tier 3 content to an audience that is 80% Tier 1.

They're pitching people who aren't ready to buy.

Reorganize your calendar by buyer stage.
Then watch your engagement quality improve.
