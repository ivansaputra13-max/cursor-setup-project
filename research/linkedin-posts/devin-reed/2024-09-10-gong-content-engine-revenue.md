# How Gong Built a Content Engine That Actually Drove Revenue

**Author:** Devin Reed
**Date:** 2024-09-10
**Likes:** ~2,600 | **Comments:** ~220
**Post Type:** Text post

---

When I was Head of Content at Gong, we had one rule:

Every piece of content had to be traceable to pipeline or revenue. Not traffic. Not social shares. Pipeline.

Here's how we built the system:

**Step 1 — Start with the sales conversation.**
We listened to hundreds of Gong calls and extracted the questions buyers asked most. Those questions became our content briefs.

**Step 2 — Write for the buying stage, not the topic.**
"What does someone need to believe before they'll book a demo?" That belief gap was the post.

**Step 3 — Distribute through the sales team.**
Reps shared our LinkedIn content in deal cycles — not as spam, as genuinely useful resources. Prospect asks "how do other companies do X?" Rep sends a LinkedIn post.

**Step 4 — Measure what moved.**
We tracked which content appeared in active deals. Content in won deals got replicated. Content that didn't — got cut.

Content teams that report to marketing produce content for awareness.
Content teams that partner with sales produce content that closes deals.
