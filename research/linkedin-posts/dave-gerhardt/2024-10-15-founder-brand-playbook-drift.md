# The Founder Brand Playbook I Used at Drift

**Author:** Dave Gerhardt
**Date:** 2024-10-15
**Likes:** ~2,900 | **Comments:** ~210
**Post Type:** Text post

---

When I joined Drift in 2016, we had almost no marketing budget.

So we made David Cancel (the CEO) famous on the internet.

**Step 1 — Find the founder's authentic POV.**
David had 20 years of SaaS opinions most people were afraid to say out loud. That's your content.

**Step 2 — Pick one platform and go all in.**
We chose podcasts and Twitter. One or two channels done well beats five done poorly.

**Step 3 — Document, don't create.**
David didn't have time to write essays. We documented what he already said in Slack, meetings, customer calls.

**Step 4 — Repurpose relentlessly.**
One podcast episode became 5 tweets, 2 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post, 1 playbook chapter.

**Step 5 — Make it about the category, not the product.**
David talked about the future of sales and marketing. Not Drift features. That's why people listened.

Result: Drift became a category-defining brand in 3 years.
The founder was the marketing strategy.
